## Tier 1 — Low risk, high leverage, ship in the next 6 months

These are direct extensions of the locked venue-partnership model or the already-built verification stack. They don't open new compliance surfaces.

### 1.1 Venue Partner Subscriptions ($99–$199/mo per venue) — already in the v0.2 spec

This is the "Groupon-style" line item you already locked. I'm flagging it here because it's the single highest-leverage non-membership revenue in the plan and the easiest to scale:

- Local brewpubs, coffee shops, trade-supply houses, IBEW/UA halls, makerspaces, coworking, hardware stores pay Werkles a flat monthly fee to be listed as a recommended in-person meet location for verified members in their metro.
- 50 venue partners at $149/mo = **~$89k/year** in non-membership revenue from one city.
- Zero securities surface — venues are paying for promotional placement, not commerce facilitation. ([Sharetribe's monetization guide](https://www.sharetribe.com/how-to-build/two-sided-marketplace/) categorizes this as "Listing fees" — standard marketplace pattern.)
- Ships at v1.0 per the existing spec timeline.

### 1.2 Featured Meet-Up Sponsorships ($250–$500 per event) — already in the spec

Same instrument, different cadence. One venue per metro per month hosts a "Werkles Foundry Night" branded meet-up with their name attached. Sponsorship covers their bar tab and a small Werkles fee on top.

- 4 metros × $400/event × 12 months = **~$19k/year** in event sponsorship at small-scale rollout.
- Stackable with #1.1.

### 1.3 Sponsored Lane Spotlights ($500–$2,000/mo per metro per slot)

This is new. Each metro's match deck has 3 "Featured" slots per archetype lane. A licensed plumbing supply house can sponsor "The Builder — Plumbing" for Columbus OH for a month. When a Werkles user in Columbus self-identifies as a plumbing Builder during onboarding, they see one sponsored card from the supply house in their first session — "Bring your own dossier. Bring your own tools. Or get 15% off your first order at Capital City Pipe & Supply."

- Sponsor pays Werkles. Werkles never takes a cut of supply-house revenue.
- Sponsor offers a discount to verified Werkles members; redemption tracking happens off-platform via discount code.
- Compliance: this is **standard sponsored content / native advertising**. FTC requires "Promoted" or "Sponsored" disclosure on every placement ([Marketplace 2.0 monetization frameworks from McFadyen](https://mcfadyen.com/2024/02/28/marketplace-monetization/) treat this as a Tier-1 expansion lever).
- Estimated revenue at scale: 5 metros × 5 lanes × $1,000/mo average = **~$300k/year**.

### 1.4 Verification Badge Refresh Fees ($10 per refresh after 30 days stale)

Already in the Spec v0.2 vendor-cost passthrough model. Funds verification staleness is real (Plaid asset reports age out), and re-verification has a real cost. Members get one free refresh per year on annual plan; off-cycle refreshes cost $10. Pure cost recovery, not profit center — but it's free money that you'd otherwise leave on the table.

- 200 Backers × 1.5 refreshes/year × $10 = **~$3k/year** at modest scale.
- Compliance: cost-recovery, not transaction-based. Clean.

### 1.5 Annual Plan Tease — "Lock the Joints" Founders Cohort

Not a new tier. A **psychological pull** on the existing $99 annual plan. First 500 annual subscribers get:
- A Founder badge on their profile (visible to other members).
- A printed Foundry Dues card mailed to them (use the asset I generated yesterday — render to print at Moo or VistaPrint, $3–5 per card including postage).
- Locked-in $99/year forever (grandfathered against future price hikes).

- Cost: ~$5/founder for the physical card + postage = $2,500 total at 500 founders.
- Revenue: 500 × $99 = $49,500 in pre-launch / early annual subscriptions.
- Compliance: no new surface. Just a marketing layer on existing membership.

## Tier 2 — Medium risk, real leverage, ship after first-metro proves out

### 2.1 Werkles Toolkit / Productized Templates ($29–$99 per template)

Werkles already plans to provide **deal-room workspace templates** (LLC operating agreement skeleton, term sheet skeleton, roles matrix) free for members. The Toolkit extends that into pay-per-template specialized documents:

- "Two-Plumber Partnership LLC Template" — $49
- "Operator + Backer JV Term Sheet (50/50 sweat-equity)" — $79
- "Worker Walkout Group Crew Charter" — $49
- "First-Year Capital Contribution & Vesting Schedule" — $99

Each template carries a prominent "**Starter language only. Not legal advice. Engage independent counsel before signing.**" disclaimer per the locked Terms of Service §10.

- Revenue: 1,000 members × $50 avg × 30% conversion = **~$15k/year**.
- Compliance risk: low-medium. Selling templates is normal. The disclaimer + the existing locked compliance posture protects you. But: attorney review of the templates themselves is mandatory before sale.

### 2.2 Werkles Verified Listings on State Job Boards / Apprentice Portals

Partnership revenue, not advertising. Werkles becomes the "verified profile" layer that state apprenticeship programs, trade schools, and union locals can plug into. Graduating apprentices get a free 1-year Foundry Dues membership pre-loaded with their verified license; the union or trade school pays a per-graduate fee to Werkles.

- Revenue: $25/graduate × 1,000 graduates from one large trade school = $25k.
- Compliance: low. This is B2B partner revenue, not user transaction comp.
- Caveat: long sales cycle. Trade schools and unions move slowly.

### 2.3 Werkles Insights / Local Trade Market Reports ($199–$499 per report)

Werkles will accumulate genuinely valuable anonymized data: "What's the average capital ask of new plumbing operators in Indianapolis? What's the median sweat-equity contribution from Backers in HVAC? How long does the average matched pair take to file an LLC together?" A quarterly trade-market intel report sold to:
- Local SBA chapters
- Trade associations (PHCC, IBEW, UA, NECA)
- Small business banks who lend into trades
- PE rollup firms (yes, the same ones you're routing around — they'll pay for the data)

**Pricing:** $199 quarterly per metro per trade, $499 for all-metros all-trades.
**Revenue at scale:** 100 subscribers × $499/quarter = **~$200k/year**.
**Compliance:** anonymized data only, never user-identifiable. Standard practice. Privacy Policy already covers it.
**Risk:** selling to PE rollups is *philosophically* in tension with the Werkles thesis (route around them). You can choose to gate this — sell to trade associations and SBA, refuse PE buyers. That's a defensible brand position and a way to convert "we don't sell to predators" into marketing.

### 2.4 Sponsored Apprenticeship Pipeline (B2B revenue)

Large trade employers (Roto-Rooter, ABM, RPM Industrial Group, Comfort Systems USA) pay Werkles to be the default landing page for their apprenticeship programs. A Werkles Worker who marks intent "Active" sees those employer ads in their feed alongside Operator ventures. The employer pays Werkles per qualified application, not per hire.

- Revenue: $25–$75 per qualified application × hundreds of applications/month at scale.
- Compliance: this is a **job board** revenue model. FCRA implications if any employer screening happens through Werkles; cleanest if Werkles is just a referral funnel and employers do their own ATS work.
- Tension: this competes directly with the Worker-walks-out-to-join-an-Operator thesis. You can run both — employers and Operators compete for the same Workers, and Werkles wins either way. But position carefully so it doesn't undermine the walkout-crew narrative.

## Tier 3 — Higher risk or longer horizon, needs attorney + product work

### 3.1 Werkles Pro for Suppliers ($299–$999/mo)

Adjacent to #1.3 but bigger. Trade-supply distributors (Ferguson, Lowe's Pro, supply houses, equipment dealers) pay for a "Werkles Pro" account that gives them:
- Direct visibility into matched ventures in their metro (industry only, never user-identifiable).
- The ability to sponsor specific ventures by offering equipment/credit terms.
- Aggregate market intel on what new operators are buying.

**Revenue ceiling:** 100 supplier accounts × $499/mo = **$600k/year**.
**Compliance:** medium. Credit-terms-offered-to-matched-ventures starts to brush against lending. Solution: Werkles never originates or services credit; the supplier extends terms directly off-platform. Werkles is just the introduction surface.
**Attorney sign-off required** before launch.

### 3.2 Equity-Free Mentor Program ($49/mo add-on for members)

A vetted pool of retired/semi-retired tradespeople, operators, and small-business veterans available for 1:1 consults with verified Werkles members. Like Office Hours but specifically for blue-collar venture formation. Mentors are 1099 contractors paid hourly by Werkles; members pay a $49/mo add-on for unlimited consults (capped at 4/month).

- Revenue: 200 members on mentor add-on × $49 = $9,800/mo = **~$118k/year**.
- COGS: ~50% of revenue goes to mentor payments.
- Compliance: mentors are **not** financial or legal advisors. Standard "general business mentoring" framing. Mentors sign a "no specific advice on securities, lending, or legal/tax matters" agreement. Same posture as YPO, Vistage, EO-style peer mentoring.

### 3.3 Werkles Insurance Affiliate (referral revenue from group policy carriers)

Verified small business operators need GL, workers comp, commercial auto, tools insurance. Werkles partners with one carrier per category (Hiscox for GL, Pie for WC, Progressive Commercial for auto) and earns an affiliate commission for verified-member referrals.

- Revenue: $50–$200 per converted policy × hundreds of conversions = real money over time.
- **Compliance: this is the trickiest of all the Tier-3 ideas.** Insurance brokerage requires state-by-state licensing. Affiliate referrals are different from brokerage but the line moves with state. Werkles must never appear to "sell" or "recommend" specific policies — only "introduce verified members to carriers, with carrier handling all sales activity off-platform."
- **Attorney sign-off required.** Don't ship without it.

### 3.4 Werkles Conferences / Foundry Days (Annual ticketed event)

Once Werkles has 5,000+ active members across 5+ metros, an annual 2-day Foundry Days conference. Tickets $299–$599 per attendee. Sponsorships from Tier 1.3 / Tier 2.4 / Tier 3.1 partners. Cinematic Mythic-Capitalism stagecraft, founder/operator panels, hands-on workshops, gear/tool demos.

- Revenue: 500 attendees × $399 + $200k in sponsorship = **~$400k per event**.
- Compliance: standard conference. No securities surface.
- Timing: minimum 24 months after launch. Ship membership first.

## What I would NOT do (rejected on compliance grounds)

These all sound great. They all break the locked posture. Listing them so the bad ideas have a graveyard:

| Idea | Why rejected |
|---|---|
| Carry interest on Werkles-introduced deals (the AngelList model) | Requires investment adviser registration. Years of regulatory build. Spec v0.2 already rejected this. |
| Pay-per-introduction (Thumbtack-style lead fees) | Pay-per-lead model converts the platform into a finder. Securities-adjacent. Blows up the compliance posture. |
| Take rate on user-to-user deals when they close | Explicitly forbidden by the seven operating commitments. Non-starter. |
| Werkles-branded business loans to matched ventures | State-by-state lender licensing. Capital requirements. Massive regulatory build. Outside scope. |
| "Premium verification" where Werkles charges more for stronger badges | The Comptroller would catch this — pay-to-play trust signal. Already rejected in v0.3 compliance review §5. |
| Cap table tooling that handles equity issuance on platform | Securities registration. Outside scope. |
| Werkles' own escrow service for member-to-member transactions | Money transmitter licensing in every state. The single biggest compliance trap. |

## Revenue model with diversification baked in

Combining the locked Foundry Dues spine with Tier-1 and Tier-2 ideas, here's a realistic 36-month revenue curve from one launch metro scaling to four:

| Source | Year 1 (1 metro, 500 paid) | Year 2 (3 metros, 2,500 paid) | Year 3 (5 metros, 6,000 paid) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Foundry Dues membership | $50,000 | $250,000 | $600,000 |
| Venue Partner subscriptions | $20,000 | $120,000 | $300,000 |
| Featured Meet-Up sponsorships | $5,000 | $40,000 | $100,000 |
| Sponsored Lane Spotlights | $15,000 | $100,000 | $300,000 |
| Verification refresh + ancillary | $2,000 | $10,000 | $25,000 |
| Werkles Toolkit templates | — | $15,000 | $40,000 |
| Apprenticeship pipeline (B2B) | — | $20,000 | $80,000 |
| Insights reports (Tier 2.3) | — | $30,000 | $150,000 |
| **Total ARR** | **$92,000** | **$585,000** | **$1,595,000** |

Membership is 54% of Year 1 revenue and 38% of Year 3. The diversification is the point: as the membership scales, the platform-economics kick in and ancillary revenue compounds.

This is conservative. The Tier-3 ideas (Werkles Pro for Suppliers, conferences, insurance affiliate) could 2–3x the Year 3 figure if any one of them lands.
