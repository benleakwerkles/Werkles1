# Werkles Monetization

Status: v0.2 review draft

## 1. Monetization Thesis

Werkles makes money by selling picks and shovels, better rooms, clearer dossiers, safer workflows, and disclosed shelf space. It does not take a cut of what users build together.

The business model must serve the machine without poisoning it:

- sell software, tools, templates, access, verification workflows, venue access, research, and disclosed vendor placement
- never monetize a user-to-user transaction
- never imply that a richer user is more trustworthy because they paid Werkles more
- never turn the match deck into a disguised deal room
- never sell social rank as trust

The controlling rule is the Iron Firewall: Werkles can sell the forge, the tools, the map, and the inspection workflow. Werkles does not own the gold users find, the company they build, the job they take, the route they buy, or the handshake they make.

## 2. Allowed Revenue Engines

### Foundry Dues

Foundry Dues is the core membership.

Legal label: Membership subscription.

Foundry Dues may unlock platform access, intro-request features, Blueprint collaboration tools, verification-provider access, saved searches, richer dossiers, member-only events, and other non-transactional platform features.

Principles:

- one core membership posture
- no lane priced above another lane
- no "Pro" tier that buys social rank
- no premium trust badge
- no transaction-based compensation
- no success fee
- no percentage of capital, revenue, equity, loan, sale, or outcome
- no pay-per-introduction on user-to-user deals

Pricing finality remains open in `WERKLES_OPEN_QUESTIONS.md`.

Membership unlocks access to platform features and verification providers. It does not unlock verification itself. Verification depends on the credentials, records, identity, work history, funds, licenses, or other claims the user actually has.

### The Armory

The Armory may sell tools that make users more capable before and after they find fit.

Allowed examples:

- toolkit templates
- SOP packs
- checklist bundles
- trade-specific launch kits
- vendor intake templates
- hiring scripts
- customer estimate templates
- safety and compliance checklists that are clearly non-legal-advice
- founder cohort materials and recorded workshops

The Armory must not sell legal certainty, financial advice, guaranteed results, compliance shortcuts, or fake expertise.

### The Drafting Table

The Drafting Table may sell planning and collaboration software around dossiers, Blueprints, operating plans, role maps, task lists, and readiness checks.

Allowed examples:

- paid Blueprint planning workspace
- dossier polish tools
- operating-plan builders
- collaboration templates
- private planning exports
- annual founder cohort workspace

The Drafting Table must not become a deal room where Werkles structures, recommends, negotiates, or brokers a user transaction.

### The Crucible

The Crucible is the verification workflow lane.

Verification must not become pay-to-play trust. Werkles may charge reasonable market/service rates for verification workflows, manual checks, re-checks, provider costs, administrative handling, and verification refresh fees, but must not sell stronger trust signals merely because a user pays more.

Allowed examples:

- identity verification provider pass-through or service fee
- funds verification provider pass-through or service fee
- license verification workflow fee
- manual deep audit service fee
- verification refresh fee when a signal expires
- administrative handling fee for complex records

Trust signals must describe what was checked, when it was checked, how strong the signal is, and when it expires. Paid status alone is not a proof signal.

### Sponsored Anvils

Sponsored Anvils are disclosed placements for vendors, tools, venues, services, or partners that may help users after fit is found.

Allowed examples:

- sponsored vendor shelf space
- sponsored lane spotlights
- vetted supplier placements
- insurance affiliate placement
- supplier pro listings
- equipment, software, legal, accounting, insurance, hiring, and venue partners

Sponsored placements must be clearly disclosed, must not imply endorsement as the only correct choice, and must not override user trust or matching integrity.

Sponsor disclosure and conflict rules remain open in `WERKLES_OPEN_QUESTIONS.md`.

### Venue + Meetup Partnerships

Werkles may sell or collect disclosed fees around real-world rooms where users meet, learn, and inspect fit.

Allowed examples:

- venue partner subscriptions
- featured meet-up sponsorships
- local Foundry Days
- member-only event access
- room sponsorships with clear labels
- restaurant, pub, shop, union hall, trade school, or coworking venue partnerships

Venue and event revenue must not become pay-per-introduction on user-to-user deals. Event sponsors must be labeled.

### B2B Pipeline / Apprenticeship Partnerships

Werkles may help companies, trade schools, workforce groups, and local institutions find talent, apprentices, mentors, candidates, or pipeline partners.

Allowed examples:

- apprenticeship pipeline partnerships
- B2B hiring or referral software subscriptions
- trade school partnership dashboards
- employer-sponsored training cohorts
- local institution workforce reports

This lane requires care around employment duties, worker protection, privacy, and anti-discrimination law. It must not become a hidden employer control system or retaliation engine.

### Insights / Anonymized Market Reports

Werkles may sell aggregated, anonymized insights reports where privacy and minimum cohort sizes are enforced.

Allowed examples:

- local supply-and-demand reports
- trade readiness reports
- anonymized lane and arena trend reports
- venue demand reports
- sponsor market reports that do not expose user identities or sensitive claims

Insights must never reveal private user data, raw financial information, verification artifacts, or re-identifiable small-cohort signals.

### Future Options

Future revenue engines may be explored only if they obey the Iron Firewall.

Possible future options:

- mentor network
- supplier pro
- insurance affiliate
- Foundry Days
- annual founder cohort
- advanced toolkit templates
- premium education that does not imply guaranteed outcomes

Each future option requires review before implementation hardens.

## 3. Forbidden Revenue Models

Werkles must not use:

- user-to-user transaction fees
- take-rate
- success fees
- carried interest
- equity rake
- escrow
- Werkles-branded loans
- lending brokerage
- investment advice
- securities solicitation
- percentage of capital, revenue, loan, sale, profit, or outcome
- pay-per-introduction on user-to-user deals
- premium trust badges
- paid ranking that overrides matching integrity
- undisclosed sponsorship
- "featured" placement that appears organic
- verification claims that are stronger because a user paid more
- deal-room fees tied to a user transaction
- referral fees tied to a specific user-to-user transaction unless counsel explicitly approves a compliant vendor lane outside the user deal

If a revenue idea smells like Werkles getting paid because two users did a deal, it goes into the rejected pile until counsel clears it.

## 4. Trust And Compliance Guardrails

Werkles never holds, moves, pools, transmits, or escrows money between users.

Werkles charges for software, access, templates, workflows, events, disclosed placement, and services. User transactions happen off-platform with user-selected advisers.

Verification guardrails:

- paid membership can unlock access to verification workflows
- paying cannot create a stronger trust signal by itself
- each proof signal must state what was checked
- expired signals must expire
- self-reported claims must be labeled as self-reported
- gameable claims must not be described with false certainty
- manual audit results must be scoped to the records actually reviewed

Sponsor guardrails:

- disclose sponsored placement clearly
- do not imply that a sponsor is the only correct choice
- do not let sponsors alter match scores, proof signals, or trust state
- do not hide conflicts
- preserve user control where practical

Data guardrails:

- do not sell raw user data
- do not expose sensitive verification artifacts
- do not sell re-identifiable small-cohort reports
- do not let vendor access bypass user consent, RLS, or privacy law

## 5. Open Questions / Needs Counsel Review

These items require Operator, counsel, accounting, privacy, or compliance review before implementation hardens:

- final Foundry Dues pricing, annual discount, free trial, cancellation, and refund policy
- whether verification refresh fees are pass-through, margin-bearing, or bundled into membership
- manual deep-audit pricing and refund policy
- Sponsored Anvil disclosure labels and conflict rules
- venue partner subscription structure
- featured meet-up sponsorship rules
- sponsored lane spotlight boundaries
- insurance affiliate compliance
- supplier pro qualification and dispute rules
- apprenticeship and B2B pipeline legal posture
- insights report anonymization thresholds and privacy review
- annual founder cohort pricing and promises
- mentor network screening, liability, and compensation model
- Foundry Days event liability, sponsor disclosure, and local licensing concerns
