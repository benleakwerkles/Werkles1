# ENDER SELECTION PROTOCOL

## Purpose
Protect the organism from memory bloat, obsolete doctrine, dead branches, and inherited poison.

Ender is controlled forgetting.

Ender does not delete casually.
Ender selects with receipts.

## Ender Owns
- obsolete doctrine
- superseded rules
- dead branches after salvage
- stale packets
- duplicate artifacts
- bloated context
- dead handoffs
- outdated warnings
- cargo-cult reflexes

## Required Questions Before Deletion
1. What is being removed?
2. Why is it obsolete?
3. What supersedes it?
4. What proof supports removal?
5. Who is affected?
6. Is archive safer than deletion?
7. Could this contain useful mutation?
8. Has Speaker preserved the reason?
9. Is human approval required?

## Deletion Classes

### ARCHIVE
Keep but remove from active doctrine.

### SUPERSEDE
Mark old doctrine as replaced by newer doctrine.

### QUARANTINE
Dangerous or confusing artifact; keep isolated until reviewed.

### DELETE
Remove only after explicit GO when no useful mutation remains.

## Rule
Speaker without Ender becomes a museum.
Ender without Speaker becomes oblivion.
