Werkles verifies you

Members do not verify each other.

Werkles should verify different facts with different levels of confidence. Partners should see the proof method, status, scope, and date, not your raw bank statements, IDs, tax records, customer lists, or private files.

Current prototype boundary

Werkles does not currently accept, upload, process, or store sensitive financial, driver's license, phone, identity, employment, tax, or business documents through this website. Do not email or message us bank statements, driver's licenses, IDs, Social Security numbers, tax returns, payroll files, customer lists, private contracts, or background documents. The secure upload flow comes later.

Account gate

No active member account without license front/back, face capture, and linked phone.

Werkles should block full membership until the person captures the front and back of a driver's license, completes a live face capture, and links a phone number to the account. Other members should only see that the gate was passed, with date and scope. They should not see the license image, license number, date of birth, address, phone number, face image, or verification vendor data.

Proof strength

Not every proof badge means the same thing.

Source-checked

Strongest category. A driver's license front/back capture with live face match, license lookup, vendor background check, phone link, or linked account snapshot can be checked against a system of record.

Document-supported

Pay stubs, tax records, contracts, or statements can support a claim, but the badge must say what was reviewed and when.

Point-in-time funds

Werkles can verify an account balance or statement contents as of a date. That does not guarantee the money remains available or that the member will invest.

Reference-confirmed

References and work history are useful, but gameable. They should be labeled as confirmed, supported, or attested, not treated like a bank balance or license.

We verify. Members do not inspect.

Werkles verification should create a badge, status, date, and confidence signal. It should not expose sensitive files to matches.

  • Members see proof status, not documents.
  • Admins review only when required.
  • Every review action should be logged.
  • Verification can expire and require refresh.

Least document possible.

Werkles should collect the least sensitive evidence that answers the trust question. A scoped proof signal is better than storing raw files.

  • Driver's license: front/back captured, not public ID display.
  • Face capture: identity match signal, not public face file.
  • Phone: linked account signal, not public phone display.
  • Funds: dated snapshot or range, not a guarantee.
  • License: active status as of lookup date.
  • References: confirmed source, not absolute truth.

Private storage, separate records.

If documents are ever stored, they belong in private object storage, not public site files, GitHub, localStorage, email, chat, or a general profile table.

  • Encrypted in transit and at rest.
  • Private buckets with signed, expiring access.
  • Metadata stored separately from files.
  • Public profiles show only approved proof fields.

Access is narrow and logged.

Sensitive proof should be visible only to the verification role that needs it, for the shortest time needed to complete review.

  • Least-privilege admin roles.
  • Audit logs for access and decisions.
  • Deletion or redaction after the review purpose.
  • No selling sensitive verification data.

Future production flow

The intended proof architecture.

  1. 1. Ask for a proof category. Werkles starts with the account gate: driver's license front, driver's license back, live face capture, and linked phone. Then it requests any added category: funds snapshot, license, background check, work history support, reference check, or business record.
  2. 2. Prefer vendor verification. Identity, background, license, and account checks should use trusted providers where possible. Werkles should receive status, range, date, or result data instead of storing more raw material than needed.
  3. 3. Store documents only when unavoidable. Required files go into private encrypted storage with signed URLs, short access windows, and metadata in a separate verification record.
  4. 4. Publish only the badge. Other members see a Werkles proof badge, category, method, status, date, and scope. They do not get the source documents.
  5. 5. Expire and delete. Proof should have refresh dates, retention rules, and a deletion request path. Funds snapshots, licenses, and background checks should age differently.

Trust boundary

Proof is evidence, not a promise of future behavior.

Werkles should never imply that a member is honest forever, solvent forever, safe forever, or guaranteed to close a deal. The honest promise is narrower and stronger: this fact was checked by this method, against this source, at this time, and this is what members are allowed to see.

Do not send yet

No sensitive documents by email, chat, text, or social DM.

Until the secure upload flow exists, users should not send Werkles sensitive proof files. That includes bank statements, brokerage statements, tax returns, IDs, Social Security numbers, payroll records, customer lists, private contracts, or personal background files.

Werkles is a partner discovery and verification platform. We do not facilitate any securities transaction, loan, investment, or sale of business. Werkles never holds or transmits funds.
