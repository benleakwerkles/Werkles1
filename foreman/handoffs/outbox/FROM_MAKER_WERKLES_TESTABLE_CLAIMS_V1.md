## Purpose

The top 10 assumptions currently embedded in Werkles, each rated **Proven / Assumed / Unknown** with the **cheapest test** to de-risk it.

Status key:
- **Proven** = real evidence exists.
- **Assumed** = believed and designed-around, but no evidence yet.
- **Unknown** = genuinely untested or unclear which way it goes.
- (Note: "decided/locked in doctrine" ≠ Proven. A pricing decision is not a paying customer.)

Honest baseline: Werkles is pre-real-user, so almost nothing is **Proven**. That's the point of this list.

---

### 1. Demand exists — people actively want partner/opportunity discovery
- Proven: no.
- Assumed: **yes** — the whole product assumes a felt need to "find who/what to build with."
- Unknown: whether it's a top-3 pain people will act on, vs a nice-to-have.
- **Cheapest test:** 10–15 problem interviews + a landing/waitlist page; measure waitlist conversion and unprompted "I need this."

### 2. Marketplace liquidity — enough complementary users to actually match
- Proven: no.
- Assumed: yes (two-sided value depends on it).
- Unknown: the cold-start — can you produce a real match for an early user from a thin pool?
- **Cheapest test:** concierge — for the first N intakes, can Ben find ≥1 plausible complementary match from his existing network/manual sourcing? If not, liquidity is the #1 risk.

### 3. The five-lane model (Builder/Operator/Backer/Connector/Spark) fits how real users self-describe
- Proven: no.
- Assumed: yes (taxonomy is baked into onboarding, copy, icons).
- Unknown: do users pick a lane confidently, or stall on "unsure"?
- **Cheapest test:** in the WoZ intake, make lane optional; measure % who self-select vs "not sure," and whether their free-text maps cleanly to one lane.

### 4. Discovery/translation works — a useful recommendation can be produced from an intake
- Proven: no.
- Assumed: yes (the core promise of the pivot).
- Unknown: can a human (let alone a future engine) reliably turn Layer 0 → a believable rec?
- **Cheapest test:** the WoZ Concierge Test — "feels right" rate + action-taken rate across 20 users.

### 5. Bottleneck framing adds value — users don't already know / can't self-solve their blocker
- Proven: no.
- Assumed: yes (Bottleneck Discovery is a headline step).
- Unknown: is the named bottleneck an insight or an "I already knew that"?
- **Cheapest test:** intake asks "what's blocking you?"; reviewer names it independently; measure "I hadn't thought of that" vs "obvious."

### 6. Trust/verification (the Crucible) is wanted and drives willingness-to-pay
- Proven: no.
- Assumed: yes (Crucible is a core surface + revenue line).
- Unknown: do users value verified claims enough to pay per-check?
- **Cheapest test:** pricing interviews + a fake-door "verify this / $X" button; measure intent clicks (no charge), no build.

### 7. Membership willingness-to-pay — users pay Foundry Dues ($9.99/mo) for access
- Proven: no (price is **locked in doctrine**, not validated).
- Assumed: yes.
- Unknown: conversion at $9.99, and whether access-not-results framing holds.
- **Cheapest test:** fake-door pricing page / "join for $9.99" intent button, or a small pre-sell to the waitlist; measure click-to-intent.

### 8. Anti-guru positioning differentiates and attracts the right users
- Proven: no.
- Assumed: yes (locked brand stance: no pay-for-visibility, anti-guru).
- Unknown: does it pull the target audience, or is it inside-baseball that doesn't move signups?
- **Cheapest test:** landing-page message A/B (anti-guru framing vs neutral value framing); compare conversion.

### 9. Off-platform follow-through — users take the intro/next action and it leads to real outcomes
- Proven: no.
- Assumed: yes (the "knock → humans take it from here" model).
- Unknown: do recommendations convert to real conversations/outcomes, or die after the rec?
- **Cheapest test:** concierge outcome tracking — of recs delivered, % acted on, % reaching a real conversation/result.

### 10. Brand resonance — dark-copper foundry + Squibb voice attracts (not repels) the audience
- Proven: no.
- Assumed: yes (v0.6 style + canonical Squibb locked).
- Unknown: does the world-building read as credible/distinct, or niche/off-putting to mainstream operators?
- **Cheapest test:** 5-second tests + reaction interviews on the homepage; measure comprehension + appeal vs bounce.

## Patterns

- **Nothing is Proven yet** — expected pre-launch; this list is the de-risking backlog.
- **The biggest risks are #2 (liquidity) and #4 (does discovery actually work)** — if either fails, the product premise fails regardless of brand/pricing.
- **One test covers many:** the **WoZ Concierge Test (20 users)** directly tests #3, #4, #5, #9 and informs #2. A **landing/waitlist + fake-door page** tests #1, #6, #7, #8, #10. Two cheap instruments cover all ten.

## Recommended cheapest path to evidence

1. Stand up a **landing/waitlist page** with fake-door pricing + message A/B → tests #1, #6, #7, #8, #10.
2. Run the **WoZ Concierge Test** on the first 20 → tests #2, #3, #4, #5, #9.
No engine, no app build required for either.
