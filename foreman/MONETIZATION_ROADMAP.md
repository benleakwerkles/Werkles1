## Roadmap Thesis

Foundry Dues remains the spine. The expansion plan bolts on multiple non-transactional revenue engines over time so Werkles is not trapped at subscription-only revenue per member.

The strongest near-term idea is the venue and meetup lane because it is already aligned with the product: people need trusted rooms to meet, inspect fit, and build confidence.

Hard rule:

- no transaction-based compensation on user-to-user outcomes
- no money movement between users
- no broker, lender, adviser, or finder posture
- no pay-to-play trust
- no user-to-user pay-per-introduction

## Tier 0: Foundry Dues

Core membership subscription.

Possible inclusions:

- create and maintain a dossier
- browse match deck
- request introductions where access rules permit
- join or create Blueprints
- access verification provider workflows
- save searches
- attend member-only virtual or local events

Possible Founder Cohort wrapper:

- early annual members get a Founder marker
- physical Foundry Dues card or similar brand artifact
- possible grandfathered pricing, pending Operator/counsel/accounting review

Guardrails:

- no premium trust badge
- no lane superiority
- no pay-per-introduction on user-to-user deals
- no transaction fee
- no success fee
- no equity rake

## Tier 1: Armory / Templates / SOPs

Sell useful tools without promising outcomes.

Examples:

- toolkit templates
- SOP packs
- estimate templates
- intake forms
- licensing checklists
- vendor onboarding templates
- hiring scripts
- basic operating rhythm packs
- safety checklists with disclaimers
- trade-specific launch kits
- partnership role matrices

Source ideas:

- two-plumber partnership template
- operator/backer planning template
- crew charter
- first-year capital contribution and vesting schedule

Review needed:

- attorney review for legal-adjacent templates
- clear "starter language only, not legal advice" disclaimer
- no guaranteed compliance
- no financial or investment advice

## Tier 2: Drafting Table SaaS

Paid planning and collaboration layer.

Examples:

- Blueprint planning workspace
- dossier polish tools
- role map builder
- launch checklist builder
- private exports
- collaborator readiness checks
- annual founder cohort workspace
- operating-plan builder
- roles and responsibilities map

Rejected direction:

- no transaction structuring
- no negotiation broker role
- no deal room tied to user outcomes
- no on-platform cap table issuance

## Tier 3: Crucible / Verification Services

Charge reasonable service or market rates for verification workflows.

Examples:

- identity verification workflow
- phone verification workflow
- funds verification workflow
- license check workflow
- manual deep audit
- verification refresh fees
- administrative handling for complex records

Source idea:

- verification refresh fee after a proof signal goes stale, framed as provider/service cost recovery, not as a stronger trust badge.

Hard rule:

Verification must not become pay-to-play trust. Paid users may buy a workflow. They may not buy a stronger truth signal than the evidence supports.

## Tier 4: Sponsored Anvils / Vendor Shelf Space

Disclosed vendor placements after fit or around relevant workflows.

Examples:

- LLC service providers
- insurance providers
- accounting providers
- legal services
- equipment suppliers
- software vendors
- supplier pro listings
- insurance affiliate placements
- sponsored lane spotlights
- trade-supply sponsor cards

Source idea:

- sponsored lane spotlights by metro, lane, and arena, such as a plumbing supply house sponsoring the Builder/plumbing slot in a city.

Rules:

- clearly disclose sponsors
- do not override matching integrity
- do not imply the sponsor is the only correct choice
- do not sell user trust state
- do not hide sponsor placement as organic ranking
- redemption or discount tracking should happen off-platform where practical

## Tier 5: Venue + Meetup Partnerships

Revenue around real-world rooms.

Examples:

- venue partner subscriptions
- featured meet-up sponsorships
- Foundry Days
- local chapter sponsor placement
- paid room packages
- food and drink partner promotions
- recommended in-person meet locations

Source ideas:

- monthly venue partner subscriptions for brewpubs, coffee shops, trade-supply houses, union halls, makerspaces, coworking spaces, hardware stores, and similar local rooms
- featured Werkles Foundry Night sponsorships

Rules:

- sponsorship labels must be obvious
- no pay-per-introduction on user-to-user deals
- local event liability and licensing need review
- venue partners pay for placement, not for a transaction outcome

## Tier 6: Insights / Anonymized Market Reports

Aggregated reports that help local markets understand demand without exposing people.

Examples:

- local arena demand reports
- trade readiness reports
- anonymized skill gap reports
- venue demand reports
- sponsor market reports
- quarterly local trade market reports

Potential buyers:

- trade associations
- local SBA-style organizations
- workforce groups
- trade schools
- banks or lenders, with careful privacy and brand review
- suppliers and venues, with clear privacy constraints

Rules:

- minimum cohort thresholds
- no raw user data sale
- no sensitive verification artifact exposure
- no small-cohort re-identification
- privacy review before launch
- decide whether philosophically hostile buyers are excluded

## Tier 7: Apprenticeship / B2B Pipeline

Partnerships with employers, trade schools, workforce groups, and local institutions.

Examples:

- apprenticeship pipeline software
- B2B candidate discovery
- trade school cohort dashboards
- local workforce partnerships
- employer-sponsored training cohorts
- verified profile layer for apprenticeship programs
- qualified application funnel where employers run their own ATS

Rules:

- worker protection first
- no retaliation engine
- no hidden employer control system
- privacy and anti-discrimination review required
- FCRA review if any screening is involved
- position carefully so Worker opportunity does not collapse into employer control

## Tier 8: Future Options

Future engines that may be explored after core product proof.

Options:

- mentor network
- supplier pro
- insurance affiliate
- Foundry Days
- annual founder cohort
- advanced toolkit templates
- local operator councils
- conference sponsorships
- vetted office-hours style mentor pool

Future option notes:

- Mentor network can be framed as general business mentoring, not legal, tax, investment, securities, lending, or insurance advice.
- Supplier Pro may brush against credit/lending if suppliers offer terms; counsel review is required.
- Insurance affiliate is high-risk because insurance brokerage rules vary by state; counsel review is mandatory.
- Foundry Days is more straightforward as a conference/event model, but event liability, sponsor disclosure, and local licensing still need review.

## Rejected Ideas Graveyard

These are blocked unless counsel and Operator explicitly approve a lawful replacement model:

- carry interest on user outcomes
- user-to-user transaction fees
- take-rate
- success fee
- equity rake
- escrow
- Werkles-branded loans
- lending brokerage
- investment advice
- pay-per-introduction on user-to-user deals
- premium trust badges
- paid match-score boosts
- undisclosed sponsor ranking
- deal-room fees tied to user outcomes
- cap table tooling that handles equity issuance on-platform
- Werkles-owned escrow

Bad-smell rule:

If Werkles gets paid because two users did a specific deal, the idea is rejected until counsel and the Operator clear a compliant replacement model.

## Counsel Review Required

Counsel or specialist review is required for:

- verification refresh fees
- deep audit terms and refund policy
- insurance affiliate structure
- venue sponsorship agreements
- Foundry Days event liability
- apprenticeship and B2B pipeline posture
- anonymized market report thresholds
- sponsor disclosure and conflict handling
- any vendor referral model with compensation
- legal-adjacent templates
- employer pipeline / qualified application payments
- supplier credit-term exposure
